Historical building across from the Hall.

The Father of the American Navy, Commodore John Barry. Barry's fame has been overshadowed by his contemporary John Paul Jones. The accomplishments of Barry are numerous, including the capture of over 20 British war vessels, quelling three mutinies, and participating in the last battle of the war aboard the frigate Alliance in 1783.

More historical buildings whose name I failed to note

It looks old, it must be important...

The bell tower of Independence Hall. Did you know that it was in such disrepair that when they signed the Declaration of Independence, they rang most of the bells in town *except* this one! That's right, the Liberty Bell was one of the few bells in the city *not* rung on that historic day.

The court room in the Hall.

The seal of Pennsylvania, I think. It replaced a picture of the king when we threw him out.

The room where the delegates spent a long summer. Benjamin Frankly spent a long time staring at the sun shaped decoration at the top of the chair in the middle of the picture. When it was all over, he worked it in to his closing speech, pondering whether it was a rising or setting sun. But after the Constitution was drafted, he declared that it was indeed a rising sun.

Laurel liked the chandalier. It was not there originally but was a period piece from somewhere nearby.

Another view of the meeting room used by the delegates. The sun-backed chair sits center stage.

Top center over the door is a statue of Ben Franklin. Wearing a toga. I'm going to go out on a limb and guess this was someone exercising artistic freedom. I'm guessing Ben wasn't a big toga guy.

*The* Liberty Bell. Or a pretty good facsimile thereof.

It was cast by two people who had never done a bell before.

The crack was which showed up was drilled out and a bolt put at the top, the thinking being that it would keep the two drilled surfaces from making contact.

Unfortunately, when they tried to ring it again, *another* crack shot up and to the right and the bell was to never ring again.
