Iteration

by Bill Wickart
"Your third wish?" demanded the leprechaun on my desk.

"I don't recall two others."

"Foolish human! Wish two erased all effects and memories of wish one. I'm busy -- wish your third NOW!"

I thought; grinned. "I wish to be irresistable to women."

He gestured precisely. "Done". "Ironic," he mused, fading away. "Same as wish one."
